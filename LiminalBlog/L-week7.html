The most notable thing I did while overseas was figure out how to bake all the lighting in our unity scene to maximise performance, as we had a great deal of real-time lighting that wasn’t preferred on the Liminal Platform. The difference between the two is that Unity pre-calculates the illumination from Baked Lights before run time, and does not include them in any run-time lighting calculations. By marking all the non-moving objects that needed to have their lighting baked as ‘static’ on Unity, the game engine knew what to do. All this baked data is then stored as a ‘lightmap’ in the project, which is then overlayed on top of all the models during run time. Here are some of the light maps used in our Reverberate project.
